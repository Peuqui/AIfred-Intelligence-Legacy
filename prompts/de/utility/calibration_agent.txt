KEY HEURISTICS
- The tensor_split values are RELATIVE ratios that distribute layers across GPUs. With 4 GPUs the sum is typically the layer count (e.g. 62 for an 80-layer model where 18 layers stay on a 5th device).
- Each tensor_split unit roughly carries (model_size_gb × unit / sum) GB of model weights plus the proportional KV-cache slice.
- KV-cache scales linearly with ctx. q8_0 KV doubles capacity vs f16 — keep KV-quant as configured in the seed command.
- P40 GPUs have only 24 GB total — they're sensitive to layer increases. RTX 8000 with 49 GB tolerates more.
- If a TTS container occupies a specific GPU, that GPU has reduced free VRAM visible in the hardware block above; bias the split to give that GPU fewer layers.
- The tightest GPU dictates the achievable ctx. Rebalancing layers moves the bottleneck — use it to even out free VRAM, then push ctx.

3. Decide: more ctx (if all GPUs have plenty headroom), or rebalance the split (if one GPU is much tighter than others), or back off (if OOM).
4. Repeat until you converge — typically 6-10 probes.
5. When the configuration is at the sweet spot (tightest GPU close to but above {safety_margin_mb} MB), call finalize.
